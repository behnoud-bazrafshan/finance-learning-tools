Nominal cash flow Present value

Bond prices move in the opposite direction of interest rates. Each future coupon and principal payment is valued using the market yield. When the yield rises, those future cash flows are worth less today, so the bond's price falls. Cash flows that arrive farther in the future are affected more, which is why long-term bonds are more sensitive to rate changes.
